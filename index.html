This is "La Vaguelette" performed by CÃ©cilia Cara and produced by Hoyo-Mix for the game Genshin Impact. Click here for the non-video version. I don't know any French, but I still love this song. The character Furina sings this song at the climax of their character arc. Furina is the mortal half of a god who split themselves in half and sacrificed their divine half to save their people. This had to be done in order to deceive the higher gods who had condemned her people to death. Part of this plan was having her mortal half pretend to still have her powers which had been given to the divine half. This facade had to be performed for 500 years. Furina was an actor, but in reality, she was always acting--pretending to be a powerful god whilst possessing no powers. After this ends, she refuses to act again; she can't handle doing it anymore. However, this song is performed during a play in which Furina takes over the actor's role to keep the play moving forward. The play and the song's lyrics mirror the events of the main story and her own personal tale within its context. Through this final performance, she is able to enjoy acting again and the conclusion of her character arc is signified by her receiving a vision which allows their owners to wield elemental powers and is only granted to people whose ambitions have climaxed. This song reminds me of this part of the story and Furina's incredibly moving character arc which is why I love this song. Throughout this list you'll see that I like a lot of songs that are attached to stories I am deeply connected with or evoke certain emotions within me. Aside from the context and feeling of the song, I also like its style. The entire region shares this style.

This is "Weight of the World" from the game NieR: Automata. This song is performed in three different versions. English by J'Nique Nicole, Japanese by Marina Kawano, and in an original language created for the game, 'Chaos' by Emi Evans. The version in the video to the left is a medley of the three languages as well as a choral accompaniment. Each of this song's versions plays at a different ending of the game. Nier; Automata has a total of 5 (4, as 2 endings occur almost simultaneously) main 'endings,' although in reality they occur in sequence and act more as chapters in the story rather than separate endings. The game is a sequel to the original Nier, now remastered as Nier; RepliCant. The game's story revolves around themes of what it means to be human and the meaning of life. The main characters struggle to search for answers to these questions as they fight an endless war whose belligerents have long since perished. This story greatly resonated with me and played a large role in the development of my aspiration to be a writer. I still get goosebumps every time I hear this song in any of its versions. The song's lyrics encapsulate the question asked by the game to the player. I considered putting a video of the final ending here, but I decided against it should you choose to experience the game yourself. I highly recommend it. Beyond the context of this song, The 'Chaos' language used in one version of this song was created as an in-universe language. The game happens so far in the future that it is theorized by then all language might have melded into one language that every person speaks. 'Chaos' is exactly that. It draws on sounds used in a variety of languages to create a language that sounds like a melting pot of every existing culture.

This is "Anytime Anywhere" by milet and Evan Call, produced for the anime "Frieren: Beyond Journey's End."
This is the non-video version. Evan Call is a prolific composer for many anime soundtracks. I particularly enjoy his work on "Violet Evergarden." milet is the vocalist for most of the tracks used in this anime and Evan Call produced all of the music. His style is very distinct and never fails to evoke emotion during the climax of a scene. This song specifically was the main ending theme for the entire show. The first half featured the first half of the song as the ending theme while the second half of the show used the second half of the song as its ending theme. This was an amazing decision as the first half of the song is more unsure and asks questions while the second half is much more confident and receives responses during the verse. This reflects the growth of the main character throughout the show and adds a wonderful little detail to something so generally overlooked as the ending theme. The music used for this show really elevates its quality. I will never forget the two episodes when "bliss" plays, Fern's fight with "Zoltraak" in the background, or Stark's assault on the dragon with "Dragon Smasher" playing. The music really amplifies the scenes and ties in both quality and context, creating music I truly enjoy.
